Tamil Nadu Assembly Election 2026 — Independent People's Candidate

Therthal Arikkai

A 100-Point Manifesto for a Transformed Tamil Nadu
No Freebies Without Foundation · No Promise Without a Plan · No Power Without Accountability
A note to Tamil Nadu: This manifesto contains no slogans, no dynasty, and no populism for its own sake. Every promise here is backed by a funding source, a timeline, and a measurable outcome. Where welfare is promised, it is earned through work or tied to a productive purpose. Where infrastructure is promised, it is costed against a real budget. Tamil Nadu's GSDP is ₹35.7 lakh crore — large enough to fund transformation, if governed without leakage. This is a plan to earn your trust, not buy your vote.

Solving the ₹40,000 crore utility crisis unlocks ₹8,000 Cr/year in fiscal headroom — the single largest financial drain in Tamil Nadu governance.
GC 02 — AGRICULTURE
Solar Pump Ownership (Not Free Electricity)
Replaces an open-ended subsidy with a one-time asset transfer — farmers gain independence, TANGEDCO loses a loss-making load, state saves ₹4,000 Cr/yr by Year 4.
GC 03 — TECHNOLOGY
Tamil Nadu AI Mission + Tamil LLM
Making Tamil the language of digital access (not just a heritage language) is the most inclusive tech policy any Indian state has ever attempted — and Tamil Nadu has the talent to do it.
GC 04 — GOVERNANCE
AI-Powered Asset Disclosure Monitoring
For the first time, corruption detection becomes systematic rather than complaint-driven — disproportionate asset growth triggers automatic investigation, not political will.
GC 05 — URBAN
Municipal Bond Market Creation
Enabling Tamil Nadu cities to borrow directly from capital markets (as Pune and Ahmedabad did) breaks the state-grant dependency that has crippled urban infrastructure for decades.
GC 06 — HEALTHCARE
Universal Health Card (Single Platform)
Consolidating 6+ fragmented health schemes into one cashless card eliminates administrative duplication, prevents denial-of-care, and saves ₹1,500 crore annually in overhead.
GC 07 — ENVIRONMENT
Tamil Nadu Green Bond Programme
A ₹10,000 crore certified green bond programme makes Tamil Nadu India's first state to fund climate action from capital markets — unlocking international ESG investor interest.
GC 08 — GOVERNANCE
Panchayat Participatory Budgeting
Giving villages real control over ₹25 lakh+ annually (not rubber-stamp approval) is the most transformative decentralisation in Tamil Nadu's post-MGR governance history.
GC 09 — LAW
Police Transfer Independent Board
In a state where political transfers have paralysed honest policing for 40 years, a legally independent transfer board is not a reform — it is a revolution in rule-of-law.
GC 10 — EDUCATION
Outcome-Linked Scholarship Ladder
Replacing attendance-as-qualification with performance-as-qualification creates incentives rather than entitlements — and ensures Tamil Nadu's education spending actually produces outcomes, not just enrolment numbers.
3 Biggest Risks in This Manifesto
RISK 01
Implementation Capacity Deficit
Tamil Nadu's administrative machine is capable but stretched. Executing 100 programmes simultaneously risks superficial delivery across all rather than deep impact in any. Mitigation: Annual prioritisation of 20 points; 5-year phased rollout with Year 1 focused on governance foundations only.
RISK 02
Fiscal Overstretch if Revenue Underperforms
The aggregate budget assumption requires 12–14% GSDP growth annually and successful bond issuance. An economic slowdown, a Centre-state revenue dispute, or a TANGEDCO crisis could collapse the fiscal plan. Mitigation: A "core 25" non-negotiable list; all other points are paused if deficit exceeds 3.5% GSDP.
RISK 03
Political Resistance to Anti-Corruption Reforms
Points 71–80 directly threaten established interests — bureaucratic patronage networks, contractor cartels, and political appointees in police. A single-party majority is insufficient; sustained civil society and media pressure is required to prevent rollback. Mitigation: Legislate these as state law in Year 1 with constitutional protection where possible.
Why Trust This Manifesto Over Traditional Parties
Principle 01
Every promise has a price tag
No party in Tamil Nadu 2026 has published a costed manifesto. Every point here names a funding source. You can verify it. You can hold it against the budget.
Principle 02
No dynasty, no debt to repay
An independent candidate owes no contractor, no family member, and no alliance partner a favour. The only obligation is to the voter — and that is enforced through published KPIs on a public dashboard from Day 1.
Principle 03
Welfare that builds, not welfare that binds
Every transfer in this manifesto is tied to something that expands human capability — education, health, asset ownership, skill. Handouts that create dependence are replaced by grants that create agency. This is not ideology. It is arithmetic: a Tamil Nadu that earns can give more than one that only borrows and distributes.
